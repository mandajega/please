Pre-Pregnancy Exercises

Exercise improves fertility and promotes a healthy pregnancy.
Discover effective pre-pregnancy workouts to enhance your chances of conceiving safely;

If you're wondering if it's okay to exercise while trying to get pregnant, here's the good news: Starting a workout routine and being active is a great step towards improving your chances of conceiving. Exercise is important for your overall health and can prepare your body for pregnancy. Here's what you should know about staying fit while trying to conceive.


Benefits of exercising when your planning for pregnancy ▼

Exercising regularly before and during pregnancy is important for a healthy pregnancy and birth. It reduces the risk of problems like gestational diabetes and pre-eclampsia. Being active helps you stay strong for labor and makes it easier. Experts, like the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists, recommend that women who are pregnant or planning to get pregnant should be physically active. Regular exercise helps prepare your body for pregnancy by toning muscles, including the heart. It reduces stress, improves sleep, and helps maintain a healthy weight. Being overweight during pregnancy can lead to complications. thus, exercising can help you loose weight and improve your fertility. It's a good idea to start a fitness routine before getting pregnant, but it's never too late to begin. Exercise during pregnancy reduces the risk of complications and eases common symptoms. It also benefits the baby's brain development and heart health. If you and your partner start being active now, it can become a healthy habit for your family. You don't need expensive gyms or strict plans. Just find activities you enjoy, like walking or swimming, and make them a part of your everyday life. Being active doesn't have to be complicated; it's about finding ways to stay active in simple ways. Remember to consult with a doctor for guidance.


How much exercise should you aim for when trying to conceive? ▼

The amount of exercise you should do depends on your current level of activity. If you have always been moderately active, you can continue exercising at the same level during pregnancy. If you exercise vigorously most days, you may need to reduce the intensity if you're having trouble getting pregnant. If you haven't been active before, start slowly and gradually increase your activity level. Experts recommend that women who are trying to conceive should aim for a moderate exercise routine of at least 150 minutes per week. This means doing about 30 minutes of exercise most days. Remember, even three 10-minute sessions can be as beneficial as one 30-minute workout. Apart from traditional exercises like aerobics, strength training, and yoga, any activity that gets your heart pumping, such as gardening or housework, is good for your body and future baby. Also remember to listen to your body and stop when you feel you can't do any more.


Recommended pre-pregnancy workouts ▼

If you're trying to conceive or already pregnant, it's important to choose exercises that are safe for expectant mothers. Before starting any workout routine, it's best to consult with your doctor for guidance and approval.
Here are some safe exercises you can consider:

  1. Walking and running:
    Both walking and running are great cardiovascular exercises. However, be mindful of your intensity and avoid activities that may increase the risk of injury. Stick to level terrain and listen to your body.

  2. Strength training:
    Weight lifting with lighter weights and higher repetitions or bodyweight exercises like lunges, squats, crunches, and push-ups can help build muscle tone and bone strength. Avoid heavy weights and focus on maintaining proper form.

  3. Pilates, barre, and yoga:
    These exercises can improve strength, balance, endurance, and muscle tone. Yoga, in particular, can help with relaxation, which is important during the conception process. However, be cautious of potential injuries and work with a knowledgeable instructor to ensure proper technique and modifications.

  4. Swimming:
    Swimming is a low-impact exercise that builds muscle tone and provides cardiovascular benefits. It also offers a sense of weightlessness and can alleviate common pregnancy symptoms like nausea and swelling.

  5. Indoor cycling:
    If you have been regularly cycling indoors for at least six months, it can be a safe and low-impact exercise option during pregnancy. Stay hydrated and take breaks if you feel overly fatigued or overheated.

Remember to listen to your body, take breaks when needed, and stay hydrated during any exercise routine. It's essential to prioritize your safety and well-being throughout your journey to conception and pregnancy.


Signs you're overdoing it on the exercise▼

If you're feeling extremely tired, irritable, experiencing muscle or joint pain, or having trouble sleeping, it may be a sign that you're exercising too much. Whether you're pregnant or not, pushing yourself too hard can increase the risk of injury. It's important to listen to your body and reduce the frequency, intensity, or duration of your workouts if you experience these symptoms. Additionally, if your menstrual cycle becomes irregular, it's a good idea to consult with your doctor, as it can affect your chances of getting pregnant. Taking care of yourself and finding a balance in your exercise routine is crucial for your overall well-being and fertility.


Tips for being active ▼

  1. Avoid sitting down too much:
    Try to spend less time sitting by walking or cycling to work, standing on public transport, walking to talk to colleagues instead of emailing, setting reminders to stand up, and taking the stairs instead of elevators or escalators.

  2. Use helpful apps:
    Apps like Couch to 5k or Active 10 can guide and motivate you to become more active, even if you've never been active before.

  3. Take a walk during lunch:
    If you have a park nearby, spend your lunch break there and take a walk. If not, simply go for a walk wherever you can instead of sitting at your desk.

  4. Walk your children to school or activities:
    If you have other children, incorporate physical activity by walking them to school, nursery, or toddler group if it's not too far. It's a great way to stay active and keep your children fit too.

  5. Use a step-counting app:
    Install a step-counting app on your phone to track your daily steps and receive rewards. It can help you see how much you're moving and keep you motivated to stay active.


Exercise and low BMI ▼

If your BMI is below 18.5, it means you may be underweight. Being underweight can have various reasons, and one of them might be exercising too much and not eating enough to replenish the energy used during exercise.

Vigorous exercise is when you work out so hard that you can't speak more than a few words without pausing for breath.

Exercising excessively and losing too much body fat can affect your fertility by preventing ovulation (the release of eggs) and may cause difficulties in getting pregnant. It can also pose health risks during pregnancy if you have a low BMI.

If you're having trouble getting pregnant or experiencing irregular periods, it may be helpful to reduce the intensity of your exercise and engage in moderate-level activities. Additionally, make sure to eat enough food to compensate for the energy you use during exercise.


vigorous exercise and fertility ▼

Most women who engage in intense exercise and have a normal or healthy BMI are not affected by infertility and can continue their exercise routine during pregnancy and beyond. However, a small number of women who do vigorous exercise regularly and have a low or healthy BMI may experience fertility issues, especially if they have irregular or missed periods.

Intense physical activity can cause stress on the body, which can disrupt the hormones responsible for regulating your menstrual cycle. This can lead to irregular periods or even the absence of periods.

If you're struggling to get pregnant or have irregular periods and engage in vigorous exercise, it may be helpful to reduce the intensity of your workouts to a moderate level. It's also important to ensure you're eating enough to replace the energy used during exercise.

If you've been trying to conceive for a year without success, or if you're not having regular periods, it's recommended to consult your doctor. They may refer you to a specialist for further tests or treatments.


Men and exercise ▼

There isn't enough evidence to suggest that excessive exercise directly causes infertility in men. However, if your partner is very active and concerned about fertility, he may be advised to reduce his exercise level. It's important to note that other factors are more likely to contribute to fertility issues.

Regular exercise is still important for your partner's overall health. Being inactive increases the chances of being overweight, which can affect the quality and quantity of sperm in men.

Both you and your partner can support each other in adopting a healthier lifestyle as you prepare to have a baby together.
